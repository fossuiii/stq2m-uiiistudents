📅 29–30 June Quantitative
Experimental Methods in Social Sciences
👩‍🏫 Destina W. Winarti · UIII
Perfect if you want to test whether an intervention actually works with proper experimental design and real statistical evidence.
You'll leave knowing how to
  • Design pre-experimental, true, and quasi-experimental studies
  • Set up control vs. experimental groups with randomization
  • Run t-tests, ANOVA, regression, and factor analysis on your data
  • Understand causality, independent & dependent variables

📅 6–7 July Quantitative
Introduction to Multi-Level Modelling
👨‍🏫 Adi Cilik Pierewan · Universitas Negeri Yogyakarta
Perfect if your data is naturally nested like students within schools, or individuals within countries and your current model ignores that structure.
You'll leave knowing how to
  • Understand why nested data needs multilevel models
  • Build two-level linear models with fixed, random & mixed effects
  • Apply MLM to education data and cross-country surveys (e.g. WVS)
  • Run exercises in R. No R experience needed at all

Perfect if you want to combine the rigor of experiments with the reach of surveys and test real causal questions on populations that matter to you.

📅 22–23 July Quantitative
Impact Evaluation: A Quasi Experimental Approach
👨‍🏫 Jahen F. Rezki & Milda Irhamni · Universitas Indonesia
Perfect if you want to evaluate real-world policies without a full RCT using Difference-in-Differences, Instrumental Variables, and Regression Discontinuity Design.
You'll leave knowing how to
  • Apply Difference-in-Differences (DD) to measure policy impact
  • Use Instrumental Variables (IV) to handle selection bias
  • Implement sharp and fuzzy Regression Discontinuity Design (RDD)
  • Move from correlation to causal claims in program evaluation
